Problem 1

Given the following within-subjects data, is \(M_D\) significantly different from \({0}\)? Use a \({2}\) tailed-test with \(\alpha = {0.01}\)

A B
0 43 57
1 69 56
2 60 48
3 40 45
4 38 37
5 58 57
6 58 40
7 59 48
8 68 52
9 70 61


Summary statistics for these data:
\[M_A = {56.3}, M_B = {50.1}\] \[n = {10}\]

Calculating the difference scores \(D = X_B - X_A\)

A B D
0 43 57 14
1 69 56 -13
2 60 48 -12
3 40 45 5
4 38 37 -1
5 58 57 -1
6 58 40 -18
7 59 48 -11
8 68 52 -16
9 70 61 -9


Calculate the Mean of the Difference Scores \[M_D = \frac{\Sigma D}{n}\]
\[M_D = \frac{-62}{10}\]
\[M_D = {-6.2}\]

A B D D^2
0 43 57 14 196
1 69 56 -13 169
2 60 48 -12 144
3 40 45 5 25
4 38 37 -1 1
5 58 57 -1 1
6 58 40 -18 324
7 59 48 -11 121
8 68 52 -16 256
9 70 61 -9 81


Calculate SS of the difference scores
\[SS_D = \Sigma D^2 - \frac{(\Sigma D)^2}{n}\]
\[SS_D = {1318} - \frac{3844}{10}\]
\[SS_D = {1318} - {384.4}\]
\[SS_D = {933.6}\]

Calculate the variance of the difference scores
\[s_D^2 = \frac{SS_D}{df}\]
\[s_D^2 = \frac{933.6}{9}\]
\[s_D^2 = {103.73}\]

Calculate the estimated standard error of the difference scores
\[s_{M_D} = \sqrt{\frac{s^2}{n}}\]
\[s_{M_D} = \sqrt{\frac{103.73}{10}}\]
\[s_{M_D} = \sqrt{10.37}\]
\[s_{M_D} = {3.22}\]

Calculate \(t_{obt}\)
\[t_{obt} = {\frac{M_D - \mu_D}{s_{M_D}}}\]
\[t_{obt} = \frac{-6.2 - 0}{3.22}\]
\[t_{obt} = \frac{-6.2}{3.22}\]
\[t_{obt} = {-1.93}\]

Calculating Cohen’s d
\[d = \frac{M_D}{\sqrt{s^2}}\]
\[d = \frac{-6.2}{{{\sqrt{103.73}}}}\]
\[d = \frac{-6.2}{10.18}\]
\[d = {-0.61}\]

The decision criteria:

\(t_{crit} = \pm{3.25}, \alpha_{two-tailed} = {0.01}, df = {9}\)

The results:

fail to reject the null hypothesis, results not significant, t(9) = -1.93, p > 0.01, d = -0.61
