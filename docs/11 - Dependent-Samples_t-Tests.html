Problem 1

Given the following within-subjects data, is \(M_D\) significantly different from \({0}\)? Use a \({2}\) tailed-test with \(\alpha = {0.05}\)

ID A B
1 16 17
2 20 16
3 17 19
4 20 21
5 15 12
6 20 25
7 17 16
8 16 16


Summary statistics for these data:
\[M_A = {17.62}, M_B = {17.75}\] \[n = {8}\]

State the Hypotheses
\[H_0: \mu_D = 0\] \[H_1: \mu_D \ne 0\]


The decision criteria:

\(t_{crit} = \pm{2.36}, \alpha_{two-tailed} = {0.05}, df = {7}\)

Calculating the difference scores \(D = X_B - X_A\)

ID A B D
1 16 17 1
2 20 16 -4
3 17 19 2
4 20 21 1
5 15 12 -3
6 20 25 5
7 17 16 -1
8 16 16 0


Calculate the Mean of the Difference Scores \[M_D = \frac{\Sigma D}{n}\]
\[M_D = \frac{1}{8}\]
\[M_D = {0.12}\]

Create a column for the squared difference scores \(D^2\)

ID A B D D^2
1 16 17 1 1
2 20 16 -4 16
3 17 19 2 4
4 20 21 1 1
5 15 12 -3 9
6 20 25 5 25
7 17 16 -1 1
8 16 16 0 0


New Summary statistics for the difference scores:
\[M_D = {0.12}, \quad \Sigma{D} = {1}, \quad \Sigma{D^2} = {57}\]


Calculate SS of the difference scores
\[SS_D = \Sigma D^2 - \frac{(\Sigma D)^2}{n}\]
\[SS_D = {57} - \frac{1}{8}\]
\[SS_D = {57} - {0.12}\]
\[SS_D = {56.88}\]

Calculate the variance of the difference scores
\[s_D^2 = \frac{SS_D}{df}\]
\[s_D^2 = \frac{56.88}{7}\]
\[s_D^2 = {8.13}\]

Calculate the estimated standard error of the difference scores
\[s_{M_D} = \sqrt{\frac{s^2}{n}}\]
\[s_{M_D} = \sqrt{\frac{8.13}{8}}\]
\[s_{M_D} = \sqrt{1.02}\]
\[s_{M_D} = {1.01}\]

Calculate \(t_{obt}\)
\[t_{obt} = {\frac{M_D - \mu_D}{s_{M_D}}}\]
\[t_{obt} = \frac{0.12 - 0}{1.01}\]
\[t_{obt} = \frac{0.12}{1.01}\]
\[t_{obt} = {0.12}\]

Calculating Cohen’s d
\[d = \frac{M_D}{\sqrt{s^2}}\]
\[d = \frac{0.12}{{{\sqrt{8.13}}}}\]
\[d = \frac{0.12}{2.85}\]
\[d = {0.04}\]

The results:

fail to reject the null hypothesis, results not significant,
t(7) = 0.12, p > 0.05, d = 0.04
